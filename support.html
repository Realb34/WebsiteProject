What is the difference from a regular computer?

Quantum computers, like the Quant Comp Pro, differ significantly from regular computers in their processing capabilities. While traditional computers use bits (0s and 1s) for processing information, quantum computers use quantum bits or qubits, which can exist in multiple states simultaneously. This allows quantum computers to perform complex computations much faster than their classical counterparts, especially in fields like cryptography, material science, and complex algorithm processing.

What does quantum mean?

In the context of quantum computing, 'quantum' refers to the fundamental behavior of particles at the smallest scales of energy levels in quantum mechanics. Quantum computers leverage principles like superposition and entanglement to perform operations. Superposition allows quantum bits (qubits) to be in multiple states simultaneously, while entanglement enables them to be interconnected in ways that amplify computing power and efficiency.
